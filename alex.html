Alexandria

Alexandria

The Pearl of the Mediterranean has an ambiance more in keeping with its neighbors to the north than with those in the Middle East. Site of Pharos lighthouse, one of the Wonders of the World, and of Anthony and Cleopatra’s tempestuous romance, the city was founded by Alexander the Great in 331 BCE. Today, Alexandria offers fascinating insights into its proud Greek past, as well as interesting mosques, the casino strip of the Corniche, some lovely gardens and both modern and traditional hotels

places to visit in Alex

Bibliotheca Alexandria

The biggest libirary in Egypt, it contains 8 million books in different languages.

Fort Qaitbey

Qaitbay Citadel is one of the most important forts on the coast of the Mediterranean Sea.

Roman Amphitheater

Built in the 2nd century AD,it has 13 semicircular tiers made of white and gray marble.

Montazah Gardens

Royal gardens of King Faruk in the egyptian kingdom. The king was the last Egyptian monarch,

St. Mina Monastery

It is the largest Coptic monastery in Egypt, by the way. Which means it is the largest in the world.

Corniche

The Corniche is a glorious 3km walkway along the harbour dotted with restaurants.
